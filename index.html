Gold Marilyn Monroe (1962)
Material: Silkscreen ink and
acrylic on canvas
Dimensions: 211.4 x 144.7 cm
Locations: Museum of Modern Art, New York

Warhol created this painting right after Marilyn Monroe committed suicide in the
year of 1962. Warhol obtained a black-and-white famous photo of her in the film
Niagara 1953 and used the photo to create several series of images, including
this Gold Marilyn Monroe. He painted the canvas gold and silk screened the
stars face in the centre. He painted her face, hair, and blouse. The colour gold
is known to signify wealth, fame, illumination, and wisdom. Compared to the
large canvas, Warhol placed Monroes face in the centre to have a large negative
space. By duplicating famous photograph of Monroe, he represented her as an
infinitely reproducible image of consumer culture and also suggests to the
reproducibility of Hollywood actors. Hollywood will always find new actors to
replace old ones.
